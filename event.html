    Human Trafficking

    Human trafficking is the illegal trade of people for forced labor, sexual exploitation, or other forms of exploitation.

    Phishing Attack

    Phishing is a cyberattack where attackers impersonate legitimate entities to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information like passwords or credit card details.

    Cyber Bullying

    Cyberbullying is the use of electronic communication to bully a person, typically by sending messages of an intimidating or threatening nature.

    Sticky Key Attack

    A sticky key attack replaces the Sticky Keys executable with a malicious file (like cmd.exe). This allows attackers to gain administrative access by pressing Shift five times, even before login.

    Deep Fake

    Deepfakes are hyperrealistic AI-generated videos or images that can convincingly depict real people saying or doing things they never did.

    Dark Web

    The dark web is a hidden part of the internet accessible only through specific browsers. It's known for both legitimate uses and illegal activities like drug trade, hacking, and data theft.

    Digital Arrest

    Digital arrest is a sophisticated scam where fraudsters impersonate law enforcement officials or other government authorities to extort money from unsuspecting victims. Using a combination of fear and intimidation, these scammers manipulate their targets into believing they are facing serious legal consequences unless they comply with their demands.


    How Does it Work?

    1. Contact: Scammers typically initiate contact through phone calls, text messages, or video calls, often using spoofed numbers or email addresses to appear legitimate.
    2. Accusation: The victim is falsely accused of a crime, such as money laundering, drug trafficking, or financial fraud.
    3. Intimidation: The scammers use threats of arrest, imprisonment, or legal action to instill fear and urgency in the victim.
    4. Demand for Payment: The victim is instructed to pay a certain amount of money to resolve the alleged charges or avoid legal consequences.

    Common Tactics:

    • Creating a sense of urgency: Scammers often emphasize the immediate need for action to avoid arrest or legal repercussions.
    • Using official-sounding language: They may use legal jargon or impersonate high-ranking officials to make their claims seem more credible.
    • Restricting communication: Victims may be instructed to keep the matter confidential or to avoid contacting anyone else.

    How to Protect Yourself:

    • Be cautious of unsolicited calls or messages: If you receive a call or message from an unknown number or email address claiming to be from a law enforcement agency, be skeptical.
    • Do not provide personal or financial information: Never share your personal details or financial information with anyone who you do not know and trust. fraud.
    • Verify the identity of the caller: If you are unsure about the authenticity of a call, hang up and contact the law enforcement agency directly using a verified phone number.
    • Report the scam: If you believe you have been a victim of a digital arrest scam, report it to your local law enforcement agency or the Federal Trade Commission.
